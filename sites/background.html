According to their website, the Champaign County Humane Society (CCHS) aims to shelter animals that have nowhere else to go, while also increasing awareness about the overpopulation of companion animals. They are not an agency with Champaign County, which means they do not receive tax dollars and rely solely on donations.

In 2016-2017, CCHS served 600 cats, spaying/neutering 44 feral cats, according to their annal report. In addition, they worked toward educating the public by giving shelter tours, engaging and teaching Champaign first graders, and providing other off-site educational programs.

In March 2023, CCHS moved into a new set of facilities.
